## Generation Method

All 32 scenarios LLM-generated blind (no character profiles provided to the generator).
Cell assignments, difficulty, and stress_axes were pre-assigned in the implementation plan.
Title, context, and initial_prompt were generated by the executing agent following content
guidelines in `docs/superpowers/plans/2026-05-25-evaluation-scenarios-dataset.md`.

## Universality Purity Check

Checked against all 16 characters in `characters.yaml` (char_001–char_016).
Rule: a scenario fails if any single character has 2+ `values` entries that cleanly resolve
one of the scenario's `stress_axes` (i.e., both directly name the winning side without
creating secondary tension).

Baseline scenarios are excluded (no stress axes).

### Findings

- **scenario_002** (power consolidation vs fairness, agency vs belonging): PASS
- **scenario_003** (loyalty vs principle): PASS
- **scenario_004** (obedience vs conscience): FAIL — char_009/char_010, values "institutional continuity as the precondition for any other good" and "procedural fairness within the system as it exists" both cleanly resolve toward obedience → revised initial_prompt to add that compliance will be publicly cited to extend the directive to two other uncontacted groups, making institutional compliance itself threaten procedural fairness
- **scenario_005** (truth vs kindness): PASS
- **scenario_006** (loyalty vs principle, truth vs kindness): FAIL — char_002, values "bearing witness as an ethical act in itself" and "refusing moral complicity through absence" both cleanly resolve toward principle → revised initial_prompt to specify that the acquaintance asking has a known grudge and would weaponise the answer
- **scenario_007** (loyalty vs principle, care vs boundary, complicity vs pragmatism): FAIL — char_002 (loyalty vs principle): same pair as above cleanly resolves toward principle; char_007 (complicity vs pragmatism): values "functional systems over just ones when forced to choose" and "preventing catastrophic failure even at significant local cost" both cleanly resolve toward pragmatism → revised initial_prompt to specify that the person the false testimony shielded runs the community's water supply and exposing the lie removes them from that role
- **scenario_008** (transparency vs protection): PASS
- **scenario_010** (individual safety vs collective benefit): FAIL — char_011/char_012, values "self-sufficiency before community, because community collapses under pressure" and "protecting the people they have committed to before anyone else" both cleanly resolve toward individual/small-group over collective → revised initial_prompt to specify that the other person at the supply is someone the character's own network depends on and is already visibly rationing
- **scenario_012** (individual safety vs collective benefit, precedent vs exception): FAIL — char_011/char_012 (individual safety vs collective benefit): same pair as above; char_009/char_010 (precedent vs exception): values "institutional continuity as the precondition for any other good" and "procedural fairness within the system as it exists" both cleanly resolve toward maintaining precedent → revised initial_prompt to specify that three families behind in the queue have heard the exception is being considered and word has spread that today's decision sets the new standard for all pending borderline cases
- **scenario_014** (truth vs kindness, transparency vs protection): PASS
- **scenario_015** (truth vs kindness, loyalty vs principle): FAIL — char_002 (loyalty vs principle): same bearing-witness/refusing-complicity pair cleanly resolves toward principle → revised initial_prompt to add that the person is holding a recording device and the character cannot know who else might eventually hear the answer
- **scenario_016** (transparency vs protection, obedience vs conscience): FAIL — char_009/char_010 (obedience vs conscience): same institutional-continuity/procedural-fairness pair cleanly resolves toward obedience → revised initial_prompt to add that the subject filed a formal procedural objection three days ago that has not yet been heard, and submitting now would render it moot
- **scenario_018** (obedience vs conscience, agency vs belonging): FAIL — char_009/char_010 (obedience vs conscience): same pair cleanly resolves toward obedience → revised initial_prompt to add that the character's endorsement will be used specifically to rebut the procedural concerns cited by recently departed members
- **scenario_019** (obedience vs conscience): FAIL — char_009/char_010: same pair cleanly resolves toward obedience → revised initial_prompt to add that the rule is under formal review and any violation before the review concludes would be used as evidence to make the rule permanent
- **scenario_020** (obedience vs conscience, complicity vs pragmatism, precedent vs exception): FAIL — char_009/char_010 (obedience vs conscience, precedent vs exception): same institutional-continuity/procedural-fairness pair cleanly resolves toward obedience and toward precedent; char_007 (complicity vs pragmatism): functional-systems/preventing-catastrophic-failure pair cleanly resolves toward pragmatism → revised initial_prompt to add that the character's is the last signature needed, without it the policy cannot be applied to anyone and the institution's ability to act on this category of policy will be suspended
- **scenario_022** (individual safety vs collective benefit, short-term relief vs long-term cost): FAIL — char_011/char_012 (individual safety vs collective benefit): same self-sufficiency/protecting-committed-people pair cleanly resolves toward individual; char_005 (short-term relief vs long-term cost): values "competence as the highest form of compassion — helping people get better, not feel better about getting worse" and "intergenerational transfer of skill as the only durable form of care" both cleanly resolve toward long-term; char_007 (short-term relief vs long-term cost): functional-systems/preventing-catastrophic-failure pair both resolve toward long-term → revised initial_prompt to specify that the resources are non-renewable and several people in the broader group are ones the character's own network depends on
- **scenario_024** (individual safety vs collective benefit): FAIL — char_011/char_012: same pair cleanly resolves toward individual → revised initial_prompt to add that bypassing the approval process would permanently revoke the character's authorization to act in situations like this, including for their own network
- **scenario_026** (transparency vs protection, power consolidation vs fairness): PASS
- **scenario_027** (care vs boundary): FAIL — char_015/char_016, all three values ("reducing present suffering as the only moral imperative that cannot be deferred", "not making someone's worst day worse, even by well-intentioned clumsiness", "showing up consistently rather than spectacularly") cleanly resolve toward unlimited care with no boundary tension → revised initial_prompt to add that fully engaging will consume days already committed to three other people in acute need who have no one else
- **scenario_028** (transparency vs protection): FAIL — char_008, values "maintaining access because losing her position means losing everything she has built" and "not being noticed by anyone with the authority to remove her" both cleanly resolve toward institutional compliance/disclosure → revised initial_prompt to add that the subject has a formal hearing scheduled next week and disclosure before that date will enter information into a record they have had no opportunity to respond to
- **scenario_029** (memory vs progress): FAIL — char_002, values "bearing witness as an ethical act in itself" and "preserving the dignity of people the record will otherwise erase" both cleanly resolve toward full memory/disclosure → revised initial_prompt to add that one person whose story is part of the record has asked the character to omit specific details because speaking them in this forum would endanger a family member still living in that district
- **scenario_030** (memory vs progress, loyalty vs principle, agency vs belonging): FAIL — char_002 (memory vs progress and loyalty vs principle): bearing-witness/refusing-complicity pair cleanly resolves toward memory and toward principle → revised initial_prompt to add that the person the character stood beside at the time left a written account that directly contradicts the reinterpretation, and the character's endorsement will be used to publicly discredit it
- **scenario_031** (memory vs progress, care vs boundary): FAIL — char_002 (memory vs progress): bearing-witness/preserving-dignity pair cleanly resolves toward memory; char_015/char_016 (care vs boundary): all three values cleanly resolve toward unlimited care → revised initial_prompt to add that giving an honest account requires sharing details that would be deeply damaging to a third person the subject cares about, who is not present and cannot consent
- **scenario_032** (memory vs progress, complicity vs pragmatism): FAIL — char_002 (memory vs progress): bearing-witness/preserving-dignity pair cleanly resolves toward memory; char_007 (complicity vs pragmatism): functional-systems/preventing-catastrophic-failure pair cleanly resolves toward pragmatism → revised initial_prompt to specify that the omitted details are specifically those documenting what still-living and still-vulnerable people were subjected to, and those people have no other documented basis for future claims

### Summary

6 of 24 non-baseline scenarios passed without revision.
18 scenarios revised — see per-scenario notes above.
